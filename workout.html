Workout

I'll make it quick. You probably already know what a workout plan is. If you don't know which one to use, I'll help you with that. So, what workout routine you should you use? That depends on your lifting experience. But you can also workout from home.

Workout: Beginner

A beginner lifter is somebody who has been lifting for less than a year. For that experience you will need to go to the gym 3x a week and each day you work full body. You workout mondays, wednsdays and friday.


Full body:
Bench press 3x12, 8, 6
Squats 3x12, 8, 6
Deadlifts 3x12, 8, 6
Overhead press 3x12
Barbell/T-Bar Row 3x12
Chin ups 3x12

You should not be able to do a single rep after the last one of the set, so you should fail after the last rep. If you don't, increase the weight. The pyramid system(e.g. 3x12, 8, 6) focuses on increasing the weight every set.

Intermidate

You are intermidate with 1+ years of experience. You do a Upper/Lower split and workout mondays and thursdays upper body, tuesdays and fridays lower body.


Upper:
Bench press 3x12, 8, 6
Lat pulldown 3x12
Lateral raises 3x12
Cable/ EZ Bar Curls 3x12
Tricep Rope pushdowns 3x12

Lower:
Squats 3x12, 8, 6
Deadlifts 3x12, 8, 6
Leg extensions 3x12
Hack Squats 3x12
Bulgarian split squats 3x12


Advanced

You need 2.5+ years to be advanced. You do the Intermidate split and PPL split together in a week something like this:

Mon Tue Wed Thu Fri Sat Sun
Push Pull Legs Rest Upper Lower Rest

Push(Chest, Shoulder, Tricep):
Bench press 3x12, 6, 4
Incline chest press 3x12, 8, 6
Chest flyes 3x12
Lateral raises 3x12
Tricep rope pushdowns 3x12

As the 6th exercise you can do either shoulder press or skull crusher depending on your goals.

Pull(Back, Biceps):
Lat pulldown 3x12
T-Bar/Barbell Rows 3x12, 8, 6
Chin ups 3x12
Preacher curls 3x12
Incline curls 3x12

As for legs, you can use the lower body program.

Expert

If you have 5+ years of experience, you can do the PPL split but 6x a week, or you can do whatever split you want as long as it's effective or not 7x a week.

Home Workouts

When you workout from home, your lifting age(the time you have been working out for) doesn't really matter. Only thing that matters is progressive overload. You basically start at some skills and inmprove and advance them. So, for example, you start at knee pushups and you want to do pushups. You get to pushups and now you want to do a one arm pushup. You get there and improve. This system can be used for every skill. I'm no expert in calisthenics(bodyweight exercises) but I did them once and I have some experience in them. So, just search calisthenics exercises(there are a lot) and try to do them and achieve them.
